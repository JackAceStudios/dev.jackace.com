The "COMPLETE" Bet in Roulette

But there's more to this bet than people probably realize. One thing that really sticks out is the discs on top of 6 of his center column bets that say "COMPLETE" on them.

I had never heard of the "Complete Bet" before so I did some internet sleuthing to find out what it was.

The Complete Bet (also known as the Maximum Bet) is a bet that is offered to high-rollers who want to make multiple bets involving a single number at a time. I think it's only offered to high-rollers because it's complicated to pay out and it usually only involved at a table where a single person is playing.

When you make a Complete Bet, your large bet is divided by 40 equal units and spread amongst the following bets:

Bet Amount Number of bets Payout Total Units Bet
Straight-up 1 unit 1 35 to 1 1
Split 2 units 4 17 to 1 8
Street 3 units 1 11 to 1 3
Corner 4 units 4 8 to 1 16
Double-Street 6 units 2 5 to 1 12
Total Bets: 12 bets Total Units: 40 units

So here's what a $40 Complete Bet on the {% include roulette-spot.html number="8" %} would look like after being broken down to its component bets:

If we look at the Equity per Spot section, we can see that a $40 Complete Bet is the same as betting $12 Straight-up on the main number, with $2, $4, and $6 bets being distributed to the surrounding numbers as "collateral damage".

Each of the 12 bets involved in the Complete Bet spreads 1 unit of equity to each number of the multi-number bet. So the 6-unit Double-Street spreads 1 unit of equity to the 6 numbers involved in the 6-number bet. You can read my post on The Distributive Property of Roulette Bets to find out why.

Typically, the bet will be a nice round multiple of 40. This is why I think Drake's bet on the {% include roulette-spot.html number="23" %} is might be $40k, not $39k. Otherwise, the unit size for his bet would be $975 and his payouts would involve him getting back Black and Green chips, which are nowhere to be seen in the photo.
